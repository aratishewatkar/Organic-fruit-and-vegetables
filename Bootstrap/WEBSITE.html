Organic farmers and food producers grow and produce food without using synthetic chemicals such as pesticides and artificial fertilisers. They do not use genetically modified (GM) components or expose food to irradiation. Animal welfare and environmental sustainability are important issues for organic farmers.Organic foods include fresh produce, meats, and dairy products as well as processed foods such as crackers, drinks, and frozen meals.Organic food is grown without the use of synthetic chemicals and does not contain genetically modified organisms. Organic foods can be fruits, vegetables, grains, dairy, and meat. Organic farms can be small or large, and they must meet strict U.S. Department of Agriculture requirements to label their food as organic.
